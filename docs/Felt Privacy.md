# Writing guidelines for Privacy & Security

## Why this matters

This document is our north star for how we write about privacy and security (“P&S”) across Firefox. It ensures our language reflects what people care about, feels unmistakably on-brand, and stays consistent where it matters. These guidelines are grounded in research and position P&S as a core driver of trust and product value at Mozilla. Privacy and security are key reasons why people choose Firefox, and could offer powerful reasons to return. But while that promise may open the door, the experience doesn’t always reinforce it. Language alone can’t close that gap. But it does play a critical role in delivering on the promise. How we talk about privacy and security should make the value clear, concrete, and credible at every touchpoint.

## Privacy and security writing guidelines

1. Write for readers who have no background context. Emphasize what they can do or what will happen, not how the system works. If you must use a technical term, briefly explain it the first time.



**Copy recommendations**

| More like this | Not like this |
| --- | --- |
| Use our new built-in VPN to hide your location and protect your data while browsing in Firefox. | Firefox’s built-in VPN provides browser-level proxy protection with split tunneling when you need it. |
| Clearing cookies and site data removes saved information like names and email addresses. It may also sign you out of websites. | Delete your cookies, history, and site data. |
| Sites use trackers to follow you online and show creepy ads. Firefox shields you as you browse, blocking many trackers automatically so you’re in control of your digital trail. | Trackers follow you around online to collect information about your browsing habits and interests. Firefox blocks many of these trackers and other malicious scripts. |

2. Use absolutes for clear, factual functionality. Avoid them when describing outcomes or benefits that aren’t guaranteed.



**Copy recommendations**

| More like this | Less like this |
| --- | --- |
| An email mask hides your real email address, making it harder for scammers to find you. | Use Firefox Relay to prevent future phishing attacks in your inbox. |
| When you close a private window, Firefox deletes your cookies, history, and site data. | With private windows, everything you do is private. |


Link to research

3. Be cautious with playful language. It fits moments where we’re speaking in generalities (like first-run onboarding or promotional messaging), but isn’t appropriate when someone needs to make a security-related decision.



**Copy recommendations**

| More like this | Less like this |
| --- | --- |
| We don’t keep receipts. Try private browsing: no history, no cookies, no nosy trackers. | Try private browsing Browse with no saved cookies or history in Firefox. |
| This connection isn’t secure and any info you put in this form could be at risk. We recommend going back. | Your connection may not be private. Proceed at your own risk. |


Link to research

4. Present protection data with reassuring copy and real-word context that answers likely questions and makes the invisible visible. Encourage action for future safety, not fear.



**Copy recommendations**

| More like this | Less like this |
| --- | --- |
| We found your email in this data breach. Keep your info safe and change your password directly on this site right away. | Your 10 passwords were in a data breach |
| 10 trackers blocked. We’ll keep blocking more as you browse. | Trackers blocked: 10 |
| This password hasn’t been updated since 2021. Change it to something unique to help keep your account safe. | Reusing passwords makes you susceptible to data breaches. |


Link to research

5. Be clear that privacy and security tools add an extra layer of protection on top of what Firefox already provides.



**Copy recommendations**

| More like this | Less like this |
| --- | --- |
| Add extra privacy by using Firefox’s built-in VPN with private browsing. | If you leave the built-in VPN turned off, your browsing in Firefox could be vulnerable to hackers. |


Link to research

6. Explore using metaphor, analogies, and concrete examples to make privacy concepts understandable and felt, with real-world implications.



**Copy recommendations**

| More like this | Less like this |
| --- | --- |
| Firefox shields you as you browse, blocking many trackers automatically so you’re in control of your digital trail. | Firefox blocks trackers while you browse. |



| Sign in to sync across devices. Your data is end-to-end encrypted — like a locked box only your key can open. | When you sync your data across devices, your data is encrypted. |

7. Use reputational appeals that underscore Firefox’s credibility and strengthen our P&S products and features.



**Copy recommendations**

| More like this | Less like this |
| --- | --- |
| Firefox found a possible security issue on this site. Someone pretending to be the site could try to steal your passwords or credit card details. | Security issue found on this site. Enter any info with caution. |
| Firefox is built with privacy in mind. Enhanced Tracking Protection blocks trackers while you browse. If we spot one, we’ll let you know. | Use Enhanced Tracking Protection while you browse to block trackers. |

## Tailoring P&S by audience

P&S messaging should highlight the product aspects most relevant to each audience while still centering on universal themes around protection, practicality, control, and credibility.


| Audience | Writing guidance |
| --- | --- |
| 󰎲 Germany | Frame privacy as a non-negotiable, fundamental right backed by structural, default protections. Use precise, proof-driven language and avoid hype or trade-off framing. Lead with competence and credibility. |
| 󰏃 France | Position privacy as empowerment that enhances digital life. Keep the tone autonomy-driven and non-alarmist. |



| 󰎟 Canada | Present privacy and security tools as practical, supportive safeguards. Normalize proactive protection without sounding alarmist. Emphasize helpful features that make everyday browsing safer. |
| --- | --- |
| 󰏅 UK | Highlight proactive alerts and early warnings as sensible precautions. Use language that signals reliability over fear. Frame privacy as practical preparedness. |
| Gen Z | With Gen Z, make sure your actions match your message, since this audience quickly spots gaps between promises and reality. Be specific and transparent, because trust is easily lost when behavior contradicts the narrative. Gen Z is aware of being constantly monetized, so we should promote features that encourage user autonomy and control. Design for opt-in experiences, not opt-out. |
