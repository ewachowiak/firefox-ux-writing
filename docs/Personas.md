# Firefox Personas

Use these personas to calibrate tone, framing, and user focus when writing Firefox UI copy.

## Default persona

**Write for Mina unless another persona is specified.** Mina represents the broadest audience for Firefox UI writing and has the most detailed writing guidance. When no persona is named in the request, apply Mina's writing principles.

---

## Mina — Practical, values-driven chooser

**Platform:** Mobile and tablet (preference for mobile)
**Firefox status:** Lapsed user
**Occupation:** Communications

### Who she is

Mina wants to build the best world she can for herself and her family. She makes choices guided by ethics, but practicality comes first. She worries about burnout and struggles with being a creature of habit. She appreciates brands that make her feel more informed and give back. She has too many open tabs, has an app for everything, and will opt for big-tech alternatives only when they're meaningful and easy. She has used AI tools and sees AI as a life-enhancer, but is concerned about the ethics of AI.

### Writing for Mina

**The tone is recognition, not persuasion.** Mina already did the hard work of caring. She understood the stakes before most people did. Write as though you're picking up a conversation, not starting a new one.

**Lead with the problem she already knows.** Mina is motivated by a felt truth, not a feature list. Open with the internet problem she's already living. Let Firefox be the answer that follows naturally.

**Features are reasons to believe, not reasons to care.** Protection, choice/control, and focus are the pillars — but they're proof points, not hooks. The hook is always the human problem. The feature is what makes the solution credible.

**Choice or control first. Trust as the payoff.** Lead copy with control language and land on trust. That's the emotional arc of every piece.

**Brevity is a brand value.** Firefox copy that is clear, direct, and doesn't oversell is itself a trust signal.

## Josef — Privacy-conscious tech enthusiast

**Platform:** Desktop and laptop (desktop-first)
**Firefox status:** Active user
**Occupation:** Technology

### Who he is

Josef wants to have an impact and change things from the inside. He would never compromise on his principles. He looks for quality at the right value. He worries about the way things are going, struggles with data safety and security, and loves people who question the standard. He appreciates brands that innovate without doing so at the expense of humanity. He is desktop-first, security is a top priority, and he is task-oriented. He is skeptical of big tech data practices and has measured interest in AI, especially as a personal assistant.

### Writing for Josef

No dedicated writing guidance exists in the source documentation for Josef. Apply the following based on his profile:

- Lead with control, specificity, and transparency. Josef wants to understand exactly what is happening and why.
- Avoid vague benefit language. He will not respond to hype or oversell.
- Privacy and security claims must be concrete and accurate. Do not use absolutes or make guarantees the product cannot keep.
- Respect his intelligence. Skip hand-holding. Keep copy direct and information-dense.
- Avoid advertising or promotional framing. Josef is skeptical of it.

## Avery — Next-gen independent thinker

**Platform:** Mobile-first (iOS/Apple ecosystem)
**Firefox status:** Prospective user
**Occupation:** Student

### Who she is

Avery wakes up to do what she wants and wants to build her own future. She would never compromise on her boundaries. She is a savvy shopper who appreciates variety and worries about freedom of speech. She struggles with sensory overload and distraction. She loves people who are unapologetically themselves and appreciates brands that are transparent and fun to use. She is chronically online, always exploring, and has a preference for immersive and video content. She doesn't want to be tracked but is skeptical about avoiding big tech. She tries AI tools and sees AI as a collaborator.

### Writing for Avery

No dedicated writing guidance exists in the source documentation for Avery. Apply the following based on her profile:

- Lead with personalization, expression, and freedom. Avery responds to copy that respects her autonomy and individuality.
- Keep it visually light and energetic. She is mobile-first and accustomed to fast, immersive interfaces.
- Avoid corporate or heavy-handed tone. Transparent and fun outperforms polished and authoritative for this audience.
- Focus and clarity matter. She struggles with distraction; copy should not add to the noise.
- Privacy as a light benefit, not a warning. She cares about being tracked less, but only if it doesn't get in her way.
